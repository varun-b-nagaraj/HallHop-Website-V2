# HallHop Brand Guidelines

> Version 2.0 — July 2026

## Brand idea

HallHop makes the space between classrooms understandable. It replaces vague, paper-based movement with a lightweight digital workflow that gives students, teachers, and school leaders shared context.

**Positioning:** HallHop is the school movement platform for campuses that want clearer hallways without turning students into dots on a surveillance map.

**Primary message:** The hallway, understood.

**Supporting messages:**

- A pass should take seconds, not a lesson.
- Visibility should create context, not suspicion.
- Patterns inform people; they never replace judgment.
- Technology should fit the school day already in motion.

## Voice

HallHop is observant, candid, and quietly optimistic.

- **Clear, not clinical.** Use ordinary school language and short sentences.
- **Confident, not inflated.** State only what can be demonstrated.
- **Human, not cute.** Warmth comes from empathy, not jokes or emojis.
- **Responsible, not fear-driven.** Never use student safety as a scare tactic.

Avoid “revolutionary,” “AI-powered,” “seamless,” “military-grade,” and unqualified compliance claims.

## Visual identity

The system combines Swiss editorial structure with the physical language of a school: paper, ink, doors, signs, clocks, and high-visibility color.

### Logo

The HallHop mark is an H constructed as two walls and an open doorway. The opening represents permission, movement, and a clear path forward. Use the mark in one flat color only. Never add gradients, shadows, or rotation.

### Color

| Token | Hex | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Paper | `#F3EFE6` | Primary background |
| Fresh paper | `#FBF8F1` | Raised surfaces |
| Ink | `#171713` | Primary text and dark fields |
| Graphite | `#5E5B53` | Secondary text |
| Signal coral | `#F05A3C` | Primary action and active state |
| Chalk | `#D9F36A` | Sparing operational highlight |
| Rule | `#D6D0C4` | Borders and grid lines |

Signal coral is the only primary CTA color. Chalk is reserved for live/ready states and never carries meaning without text.

### Typography

- **Display:** Calistoga — editorial headlines and short statements.
- **Text:** Inter — interface, body copy, and labels.
- **Data:** JetBrains Mono — timestamps, metrics, and operational labels.

### Motion

Motion explains flow: a doorway opens, a line advances, a record changes state. Use transform and opacity, keep micro-interactions between 150–300ms, and remove nonessential motion when `prefers-reduced-motion` is enabled.

### Imagery

Prefer product-native diagrams, documentary school photography, architectural crops, and real artifacts. Never use synthetic portraits, floating glass dashboards, glowing blue backgrounds, or generic AI classroom scenes.
